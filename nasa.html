NASA Ames

Supporting the ISS & Human Spaceflight

NASA Ames Research Center in Mountain View, CA has the world's largest wind tunnel, an enormous 1930s airship hangar, and a cannon that shoots fire hotter than the sun [1]No, really. They use it to test heat shields.. The scientists on base study aviation, spaceflight, and information technology. As part of the HCI Group, I help make missions safer by designing software for the International Space Station and human space flight programs [2]Particularly the new Orion capsule and SLS rocket..

Safer Missions Through Human-Centered Design

Going to space is very complicated [3]Citation needed.. The smart people of NASA have to juggle tons of information to make sure the pieces of our spacecraft fit together, we don't kill our astronauts, and that we have a plan for everything that might go wrong [4]Gotta make sure that space espresso machine keeps working..

I primarily work on the Mission Assurance System, a platform NASA uses to track mission critical data. I help make our spacecraft safer as well as make life easier for the folks on the ground. I do user research, run usability tests, create prototypes and write design specifications. We're a small shop in the HCI Group. So I also manage projects, run QA tests, provide customer support, and administer systems.

It's not exactly the CIA, but I'm still working to figure out what I can and can't show publicly. I'll update this piece once I have some answers.
