About

I’m UX designer, research and strategist currently working at 18F. We’re a team of technologists making government websites work better for the people who need them most. I’ve worked with the IRS on Direct File, the Forest Service on online permitting, the Federal Permitting Council on their dashboard, and several other federal agency partners.

Before this I was at NASA Ames working on mission safety software that supported the International Space Station and Artemis. It turns out there’s a lot of paperwork to go to space! My team designed and built tools to make it all much easier to track.

I spent some time in enterprise before I moved to gov tech. I was at Underwriters Laboratories working on interfaces to help ensure consumer product safety. And before that, I was at Fino Consulting designing iPad apps that helped businesses save money on energy.

I’m big on participant consent and ethical research practices, evidence-based decision making, and accessibility, whether that’s in the products I design or how I conduct my craft. I’m pretty good at making people feel at ease (from partners to interview participants), connecting the dots between lots of disparate data, and asking tough questions to help get partners aligned.

I play percussion with the San Jose Metropolitan Band, and the Nova Vista Symphony. I also enjoy hiking, photography cars, playing city builder video games, woodworking, and traveling the world.
